01 · Before your consultation

We’ve already read your skin’s story.

Every new patient begins with a fifteen-minute conversation — on the phone or in our front room — before any examination is booked. We'd rather know what you're hoping for than guess from a form.

02 · Behind the glass door · 60 quiet minutes

Mapping, dermatoscopy, a plan in your hand.

Intraoral scan. Photographs. A slow, honest conversation about what we'd recommend and, more importantly, what we'd leave alone. You leave with a printed plan — not a quote ambushing you at reception.

03 · The year after

Your mole review cycle. Your clinician’s direct line.

Your next visit is a full-body remapping, overlaid on the first. Our dermatoscopy software flags anything that has changed, and a short letter goes to you (and your GP, if you want) in plain English.

Our approach

Look. Listen. Loop.

01 · Look

Look first.

We haven't taken a putty impression in eight years. Every mouth that sits in our chair is captured with a handheld dermatoscope dermatoscope — three minutes, no gagging, a millimetre-true digital twin on screen before you've finished your water.

  • handheld dermatoscope wireless scanner · 20 µm accuracy
  • Photographic baseline kept on your file
  • Year-on-year overlay comparison
02 · Listen

Listen next.

Eczema, acne and rosacea are diagnosed as much in the history as in the skin. We spend fifteen minutes before any examination asking about flares, triggers, what you’ve tried, what you haven’t. Trigger maps live on paper, not in your head.

  • Fifteen-minute history on first visit
  • Trigger map drawn together
  • Written summary you take home
03 · Loop

Loop, always.

Dermatology is a long game. Severe eczema is reviewed at week four, not “if it doesn’t improve”. Moles get mapped every twelve months. Isotretinoin is held on a rail of bloods and check-ins. The follow-up calendar leaves with you on day one.

  • Review dates diarised on exit
  • Annual mole-mapping recall, automatic
  • Monthly bloods for isotretinoin supervision
