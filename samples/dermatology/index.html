01 · Before your consultation

We’ve already read the rest of your skin’s story.

Your intake form isn’t a box-tick. A nurse reviews it the day before, cross-references medication, flags photo sensitivity risks, and pre-prints the dermatoscopy map. By the time you sit down, we’ve already spent forty minutes on you.

02 · In the room · 38 minutes

Mapping, dermatoscopy, a plan in your hand.

A proper full-body exam under polarised light. Suspicious lesions photographed and logged to the atlas. If something needs biopsy, we do it the same visit. You leave with a printed written plan — not a portal link.

03 · The year after

Your mole review cycle. Your clinician’s direct line.

Annual mapping recall, quarterly check-ins if flagged, and a direct secure line to your consultant — not a call centre. We stay with you as the skin changes; that’s the entire point.

Our approach

Look. Listen. Loop.

01 · Look

The visual exam, unhurried.

Polarised dermatoscopy on every lesion of concern, total-body photography the first visit, and a written atlas that lives with you. No phone torches, no rushed glance, no “looks fine” without a photo to prove it.

  • Full-body photographic mapping · 72 regions
  • Sequential digital dermatoscopy, stored encrypted
  • Biopsy decisions made with you, not to you
02 · Listen

History, triggers, the rest of life.

Skin is a diary. We ask about new moisturiser, new partner, new job, new medication — because all of it shows up on the epidermis eventually. A trigger map is part of every consultation, paper copy handed over.

  • Thirty-eight-minute first consultation
  • Trigger mapping for flare-prone skin
  • Written plan, plain English, paper copy
03 · Loop

The follow-up that follows through.

Dermatology is a longitudinal specialism, not a one-off visit. Your consultant’s direct secure line, annual recall for mapping, and a quarterly check-in if anything was flagged. We chase you, not the other way around.

  • Annual recall letter, posted and emailed
  • Direct secure line to your consultant
  • Histology reviewed with you in person, not by email
