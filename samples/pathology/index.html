01 · From slide to screen

Specimen in by noon. Slide under glass by four.

Tissue is grossed, embedded in paraffin, sectioned, H&E-stained and whole-slide-scanned on site. No courier hand-offs, no off-site processing — the chain of custody stays inside this building.

02 · At the microscope

Careful light. Unhurried eyes.

A named consultant sits with each case at the eyepiece — then again on the whole-slide image for measurement, annotation and IHC correlation. Difficult cases go to a second pair of eyes before they leave the room.

Our approach

Prepare. Examine. Report.

01

Prepare.

Tissue is accessioned, grossed and described the moment it arrives. Cassettes cycle through our on-site processor overnight; sections are cut at 3–4 microns and H&E-stained in the morning. Every slide is whole-slide-scanned before it reaches a microscope.

  • On-site grossing · no courier hand-offs
  • H&E + IHC under one roof
  • 3DHISTECH slide scanner, 40x
02

Examine.

A named consultant reads the case at the eyepiece and on the whole-slide viewer. Difficult or ambiguous findings are shown to a second pathologist in the room — not couriered out, not deferred. The microscope is still where the truth lives.

  • Dual-read protocol on difficult cases
  • Eyepiece + whole-slide imaging
  • IHC ordered and read in-hour
03

Report.

Reports are written in plain English, structured for the referring clinician, and signed by a named pathologist. The consultant’s direct line sits at the bottom of every report. If it matters, we pick up the phone before the report leaves.

  • Structured, plain-language reporting
  • Direct consultant line on every page
  • Critical findings phoned through first
