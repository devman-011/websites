Our approach

Image.
Interpret. Inform.

01 · Image

Acquire it properly the first time.

Protocols built for the clinical question, not the scanner's default. 3T MRI with dedicated coils for knee, shoulder, wrist, prostate and cardiac. Low-dose CT with iterative reconstruction. Dynamic ultrasound, not a single still.

  • → Radiographers qualified a minimum of five years
  • → Protocols agreed with the reporting consultant
  • → Repeat sequence, not repeat scan, if needed
02 · Interpret

Read by a named FRCR consultant.

No teleradiology contract. No overnight outsourcing. Your study is read on-site by the Prism consultant whose subspecialty matches your question — neuro, MSK, breast, or cardiothoracic. Double-reporting available for breast and prostate.

  • → 100% of reports signed by an FRCR consultant
  • → Subspecialty matched to modality + body part
  • → Addendum access for referrers within 48h
03 · Inform

A report you can actually act on.

Direct to you and to your referring clinician, via secure email or NHS.net. Written to answer the question, with a clear conclusion up top. Urgent or unexpected findings are phoned through the same working day, before the report lands.

  • → 24h average turnaround
  • → Same-day urgent slots available
  • → Images on disc, USB or PACS push on request

Before you scan

Six fair
questions.

Can't find your question? Call reception on 020 7946 8855 or email hello@prismdx.co. If a consultant needs to call you back, they will — usually the same day.

  • For most MRI, CT and interventional scans, yes — a referral (GP, consultant or allied health professional) helps us choose the right protocol. Ultrasound and DEXA self-pay can be booked without one. If you're not sure, call reception and we'll advise.

  • Next-day slots are the norm for MRI, CT and ultrasound. Same-day urgent slots are kept open for referrers. Weekend lists run Saturday and Sunday, 08:00 – 18:00. Mammography and DEXA typically within 2–3 working days.

  • Every study is read on-site by a named Prism FRCR consultant, subspecialty matched to the body part. No teleradiology contracts, no overnight outsourcing, no anonymous reports. The signature on your report belongs to the person who read your scan.

  • Yes — Bupa, AXA Health, Aviva, Vitality, WPA and Cigna, alongside most self-pay patients. Please bring your pre-authorisation code. If you're unsure about cover, call reception before booking and we'll check in a few minutes.

  • Yes. With your consent, we send the final report to your GP or referring clinician by secure email or NHS.net — usually within 24 hours. You receive a copy on the same day. Urgent findings are phoned to the referrer before the report is issued.
