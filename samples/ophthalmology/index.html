01 · Before the chair

A hall that asks you to slow down.

Warm paper walls, a low lamp, and an orthoptist who introduces herself. We take history in writing, on a board, so nothing is forgotten between rooms.

02 · The slit-lamp

Careful light, slow answers.

Biomicroscopy is a craft. Your consultant will move the beam four times, change magnification twice, and only then speak. Every finding is drawn into the record before anything is said aloud.

03 · Seven days later

Your file returns to the desk.

A written report, a plan, and a second reading if anything was uncertain. We ring you at seven days, not thirty — because eyes change quickly and questions rarely keep.

Our approach

Refract. Reveal. Review.

01 · Refract

Twice, on purpose.

Every prescription at Aperture is taken twice, by two people, thirty minutes apart. A subjective refraction done quickly is a prescription that travels home with you in error. We check ourselves, openly.

  • Retinoscopy first, then subjective
  • Autorefractor as an independent cross-check
  • Cycloplegic refraction for under-18s
  • Written Rx handed over and explained aloud
02 · Reveal

Slit-lamp, then OCT.

The slit-lamp examination is unhurried. We draw what we see into the record before OCT imaging confirms it. Instruments follow the clinician, not the other way around.

  • Full anterior & posterior biomicroscopy
  • Dilated fundus examination for first visits
  • OCT macula & optic nerve (Heidelberg Spectralis)
  • Optional fundus photography & angiography
03 · Review

A written report, a second reading.

You leave with a plan, and a week later you receive a posted report. Anything uncertain is read a second time by a colleague — not for liability, but because eyes deserve two pairs.

  • Posted report within forty-eight hours
  • Second-reader protocol on all complex findings
  • Seven-day call for first-time visits
  • Twelve-month follow-up window booked at discharge
