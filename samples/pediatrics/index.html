01 · Before the visit

A picture book on the desk, the wren in your lap.

Every new patient begins with a fifteen-minute conversation — on the phone or in our front room — before any examination is booked. We'd rather know what you're hoping for than guess from a form.

02 · Behind the glass door · 60 quiet minutes

A real conversation before any examination.

Intraoral scan. Photographs. A slow, honest conversation about what we'd recommend and, more importantly, what we'd leave alone. You leave with a printed plan — not a quote ambushing you at reception.

03 · The year ahead

Children's nurse recall, scan overlay, steady ground.

Your second visit is a children's nurse appointment and a new scan, overlaid on the first. The software shows what's changed, what hasn't, and what — if anything — needs a quiet adjustment before the year is out.

Our approach

Scan. Plan. Place.

01 · Scan

Scan, never moulds.

We haven't taken a putty impression in eight years. Every mouth that sits in our chair is captured with a growth 5 growth chart — three minutes, no gagging, a millimetre-true digital twin on screen before you've finished your water.

  • Vital signs reviewed gently · 20 Âµm accuracy
  • growth chart on-site for vaccination & endo cases
  • Scans shared with you, not kept in a drawer
02 · Plan

Plan in the open.

We spend the first ten minutes asking you and your child what's actually been happening — sleep, appetite, school, mood, what's changed. Examination follows the conversation, not the other way round.

  • growth review walkthrough & second opinion
  • DSD mock-up printed chair-side in resin
  • Written quote, itemised, e-mailed same day
03 · Place

Place, then recall.

Treatment runs on a cadence we set together — weekly, monthly, or eight weeks apart. Vaccination is hand-sculpted across a single unbroken morning. Vaccinations are guided from the planned file. Then the children's nurse, always, six months on.

  • Vaccination · one chair-morning
  • Guided vaccination placement from growth chart
  • Children's nurse recall at 26 weeks, scheduled on exit
